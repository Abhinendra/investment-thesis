# Indian New-Energy Stocks: High-Level Summary

Date: 2026-05-31

This is a high-level research summary for a 3-5 year aggressive investment basket in Indian listed new-energy stocks. It is not investment advice. The detailed working note is in `new-energy-stock-scorecard.md`.

## Investment Theme

India's new-energy opportunity is driven by four structural trends:

- Solar manufacturing localization through ALMM, domestic cells, PLI, and import substitution.
- EV adoption in two-wheelers, supported by falling battery costs and improving product quality.
- Battery energy storage systems (BESS) becoming important for renewable grid integration.
- China+1 and geopolitics creating opportunities for credible Indian manufacturers.

The focus is on relatively cleaner new-energy listed entities, avoiding legacy-heavy companies where new energy is only a small side business.

## Why This Allocation

- Solar manufacturing gets the highest weight because policy support, revenue visibility, and profitability are already visible.
- EV exposure is meaningful but split between Ola's higher optionality and Ather's better execution quality.
- ACME adds BESS/FDRE exposure without relying only on solar manufacturing.
- NTPC Green is kept small because it has strong strategic value but weaker current returns and stretched valuation.
- Amara Raja is excluded from the core basket because it is not pure play; lead-acid remains the main business, and lithium cell technology is licensed through a Gotion-linked arrangement.

## Key Risks

- Valuation risk: many new-energy stocks already price in strong execution.
- Margin risk: solar margins can compress due to raw material costs, global oversupply, and mix changes.
- Working capital risk: high growth can consume cash through inventory and receivables.
- EV execution risk: service quality, product reliability, volume recovery, and discounting matter.
- Battery/cell risk: owning cells can be a moat only if yield, cost, safety, and scale are proven.
- Debt risk: IPP/BESS companies can grow capacity but still destroy value if debt costs exceed project returns.
- Policy/geopolitics risk: China, US tariffs, ALMM, PLI, import duties, and localization rules can change economics quickly.

## What To Track Quarterly

- Premier: cell capacity ramp, ALMM-led demand, margins, debt/capex.
- Waaree: working capital, FCF, US capacity, EBITDA guidance, backward integration.
- Ola: volume recovery, service quality, gross margin excluding PLI, cell yield/cost, operating cash flow.
- Ather: market share, gross margin, EBITDA loss reduction, local cell sourcing.
- ACME: BESS execution, DSO, refinancing cost, PPA-backed capacity, debt/EBITDA.
- NTPC Green: capacity additions, BESS economics, debt growth, ROE/ROCE improvement.

## Practical Approach

Build positions in tranches:

- First 25%: only after valuation comfort.
- Next 25%: after 1-2 quarters of execution proof.
- Next 25%: on drawdowns where thesis remains intact.
- Final 25%: only when cash flow, margins, or scale economics are proven.

For a 3-5 year horizon, the aim is not to buy everything immediately. The aim is to own the likely winners while keeping enough cash to add when volatility creates better entry points.
